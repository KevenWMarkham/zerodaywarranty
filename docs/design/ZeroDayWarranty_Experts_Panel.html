Zero Day Warranty Agentic Scenario

Experts panel — flush out the design & validate nothing is missing
From: Keven Markham · Deloitte's Microsoft Technology & Services Practice Date: June 2026 Reviews: Architecture · Swim Lanes · Persona Portals · Azure Deployment Scope: discovery-stage RnD pilot design
Executive summary · the one paragraph

Ten domain experts reviewed the Zero Day Warranty design (architecture, capabilities, persona portals, and the self-contained Azure deployment). The consensus: the design is sound and complete enough to proceed with a discovery-stage RnD pilot. It carries the right spine — per-VIN medallion, a 24-step agent chain, a single HITL gate, and a hash-chained audit ledger. The panel raised 12 items to close before a stage/production deployment, of which 4 are High severity: a model-evaluation harness, multiple-testing correction on the cohort statistics, a CI/CD pipeline, and private networking + DR for the audit ledger. None are blockers for RnD; all are tracked in the gap log below.

01How the panel works

Each expert reviewed the design from one discipline, recorded what is covered and what is a gap, and gave a verdict. The findings roll up into a coverage matrix (§03) and a prioritized gap log (§04). Statuses:

Covered in the current design · Partial present but needs work · Gap missing, needs adding · RnD-scoped deliberately deferred to stage/prod.

Raj Patel

Cloud Platform / Infra Architect

Is the Azure footprint clean, isolated, and reproducible?

  • Self-contained single-RG design — clean blast radius, single-delete teardown.
  • Subscription-scoped Bicep with deterministic unique-suffix naming; outputs wired.
  • No CI/CD: image build/push and az deployment are manual. Add a pipeline before stage.
  • No private networking yet (public endpoints + RBAC) — acceptable for RnD, harden for prod.

AB

Aisha Bello

Security Architect

Least privilege, no standing secrets, tamper-evidence.

  • Keyless throughout — managed identity + RBAC; AOAI local auth disabled; no keys in config.
  • KV with purge protection; audit ledger HMAC-signed and hash-chained.
  • DB still uses a password connection string — move to Entra DB auth for prod.
  • No secret-rotation policy on the signing key / db-url; add rotation + alerting.
Verdict · conditional
MR

Marco Rossi

Data Architect / Engineer

Is the per-VIN join trustworthy at plant scale?

  • Medallion + per-VIN Gold view is the right shape; indexes on VIN/lot/week present.
  • PII/PHI tokenization at the Bronze→Silver boundary is named in the architecture but not yet in the SQL — add it.
  • Burstable B1ms Postgres won't hold 5,000 vehicles/week × 4 domains; size up or move to Fabric for scale.
  • No data-quality / freshness checks or dead-letter handling on ingestion.

  • No evaluation harness — add an eval set + offline scoring; wire the audit-row feedback loop into eval cases (APEX pattern).

05Round-table synthesis

Consensus verdict

Proceed with the discovery-stage RnD pilot. The design's spine — four-domain per-VIN medallion, 24-step chain, single HITL gate, hash-chained audit ledger, self-contained Azure footprint — is sound and internally consistent across all four design documents. The gaps are the expected maturity deltas between an RnD pilot and a production deployment, not flaws in the approach.

Before stage / production — close the four High items

(1) a model-evaluation harness, (2) multiple-testing correction on the cohort statistics, (3) a CI/CD pipeline, and (4) audit-ledger durability + private networking. Items 5–12 follow on the stage track. Each currency figure must also be re-derived against the client's actual baseline in a Business Value Assessment.

06Validated complete · the "not missing anything" checklist

What the panel confirmed is present and coherent across the design pack:

  • The four data domains, the per-VIN Gold join, and the medallion mapping (laptop → Postgres → Fabric).
  • The 24-step / 7-cluster agent chain, each step owned by an agent and emitting an audit row.
  • A single, enforceable human-in-the-loop gate before any external effect, with SLA + escalation.
  • A 14-field, append-only, hash-chained audit ledger — one row per step, regulator-replayable.
  • The regulatory step (NHTSA EWR) and the supplier-chargeback evidence package.
  • Per-persona views (nine portals) and the full capability swim lanes.
  • A self-contained, reproducible Azure deployment (single RG, IaC, identity, secrets, observability).
  • Clear labelling of all currency figures as synthetic reference-scenario values, with parameterized inputs.

The items added as a result of this review — observability in the deployment, and the gap log above — are now reflected in the Azure Deployment document and the IaC.
