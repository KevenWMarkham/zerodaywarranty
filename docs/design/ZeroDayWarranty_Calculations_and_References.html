How the calculations are made & the references behind them
Audience: Chris Crotts From: Keven Markham · Deloitte's Microsoft Technology & Services Practice Date: May 2026 Status: Discovery · pre-engagement Companion to: Email of same date
Executive summary · the one paragraph

A single AI agent joins connected-vehicle warranty claims back to factory build history per VIN, surfacing the statistically significant cohort × station × tool × supplier-lot interactions that drive a warranty cluster. Today this kind of root-cause investigation takes 8–12 weeks across six teams; the agent does it in under 20 minutes. In a reference scenario, the same approach identified a single supplier lot accounting for $4.2M in warranty costs over three build weeks and produced a chargeback evidence package worth $2.8M in recovery — a ~340% improvement over the manual chargeback baseline.

01What this document does

Every number cited in the discovery email has a calculation behind it and a source. This document lays both out. The figures are reference-scenario figures — synthetic, derived from publicly-documented industry benchmarks and a worked plant-scale model — not claims about Toyota's current cost base. Their purpose is to make the order-of-magnitude and the unit-economics legible before a discovery call.

Methodology disclosure

All currency figures in §2 and §3 below are reference-scenario figures unless explicitly cited as industry averages. They use a representative high-volume plant model (~5,000 vehicles/week, ~$300 industry-average warranty cost per vehicle, US-loaded labor of ~$200/hour for cross-functional RCA teams). Toyota-specific figures are not used or implied. The scenario's structural claims — agent chain length, current-state effort, automation coverage — are sourced and cited in §8.

02The four data domains the agent reads

The agent is only as useful as the data plumbing underneath. Four domains are required — all four are already concepts Toyota's manufacturing and connected-vehicle organizations work in today.

The vehicle build record

Every VIN's complete factory build history.

When the vehicle was built, where (which plant, which line), on which station, with which tool, on which shift, by which operator, and from which supplier lot — for every installed part.

Connected vehicle warranty data

Field claims and failure modes tied back to VIN.

Warranty claim filings from dealers, fault codes and telemetry from connected vehicles, customer complaint categorization, and time-from-build-to-claim per VIN.

Quality events on the line

Every inspection, measurement, and defect captured during the build.

Statistical process control measurements, dimensional inspections, visual-system catches, end-of-line audits, and any holds or rework — at the per-station / per-VIN level.

Assembly line telemetry

Equipment state, throughput, and asset events from the production floor.

Tool torque-and-angle traces, robot cycle times, conveyor speeds, fixture changeovers, environmental conditions (humidity, temperature), and equipment maintenance events.

The agent's value is in composing these four domains into a single per-VIN view. Each domain individually already exists somewhere in most automotive OEM data estates today; what's typically missing is the per-VIN joinable view across all four, governed end-to-end.

03Calculation 1 · the current-state baseline (8–12 weeks · 6 teams)

When a warranty claim cluster emerges and a manual RCA fires, here's the typical cross-functional cost. The 8–12 week wall-clock figure cited in the email decomposes as follows.

Calculation A · cost per manual warranty RCA investigation

Total hours × loaded labor rate:

440 hours × $200/hour = $88,000 per investigation

For a plant running ~10 such investigations/year: 10 × $88,000 = $880,000 in RCA labor cost annually, before any warranty recovery, recall scoping, or process changes are even queued.

The 8–12 week wall-clock — why so long?

440 hours of work does not equal 11 calendar weeks of effort. The wall-clock figure compounds three factors:

  • Sequencing. IT cannot extract the data until Warranty has scoped the claim cohort; Plant Ops cannot pull build records until IT delivers the VIN list; Supplier Quality cannot engage the vendor until Manufacturing Engineering identifies the suspect process. Each handoff adds 3–7 days of queue time.
  • Cross-system reconciliation. Warranty claims live in one system, build records in another, supplier lot data in a third, quality measurements in a fourth. Manual joining is the largest single time-sink.
  • Statistical defensibility. Building a chargeback evidence package that will hold up in supplier negotiations or NHTSA submissions requires statistically significant cohort analysis. That typically means analyst-quality work, reviewed by quality leadership, before any external claim is made.

04Calculation 2 · the agentic-state delivery (~minutes · 24-step chain)

The same investigation, automated end-to-end as a single agent chain, runs in under 20 minutes wall-clock. The chain composes 24 discrete steps across the four data domains.

The 24-step agent chain (representative)

01Receive warranty claim cluster signal (volume threshold breach)
02Scope the claim cohort by part, failure mode, severity
03Pull VIN list for affected cohort
04Join VIN list to build records (per-VIN factory history)
05Extract build-week distribution of affected VINs
06Identify over-represented build weeks vs. baseline
07Within hot build weeks: extract station / tool / shift distribution
08Statistical test: cohort × station × tool × shift interactions
09Join to quality event records (inspections / measurements)
10Identify SPC anomalies preceding the hot build weeks
11Join to assembly telemetry (tool traces, equipment state)
12Correlate tool calibration drift with hot-station defects
13Extract supplier lot codes installed in hot VIN population
14Compute supplier-lot warranty rate vs. baseline
15Statistical test: supplier-lot attribution significance
16Rank cohort × station × supplier-lot interactions by significance
17Generate root-cause hypothesis with confidence intervals
18Build evidence package: cohort, statistical tests, raw data
19Compute chargeback dollar exposure per supplier lot
20Generate supplier chargeback documentation
21Trigger NHTSA Early Warning Reporting check (if applicable)
22Route to Quality Director for human review & approval
23Write decision & rationale to audit ledger
24Notify downstream owners (CAPA, dealer advisories)
Calculation B · agent chain wall-clock

Per-step latency × step count:

24 steps × ~30 seconds average per step = ~12 minutes

Wall-clock varies with data freshness and statistical-test runtime; the 8–20 minute range covers normal-case execution. The human-review step (step 22) is excluded from the wall-clock figure since it is not the bottleneck.

SourceThe 24-step chain length is sourced from the Toyota AgenticAI OnePager scenario S1 ("Warranty root-cause investigation, 24-step chain"). The per-step latency is derived from comparable agent-orchestration benchmarks on the Microsoft platform (Microsoft Agent Framework on Azure AI Foundry agent chains with Microsoft Fabric Gold-view tools — see §8).

Attributable amount × agentic recovery rate:

Baseline recovery → agentic recovery:

If a calculation needs to be re-derived for Toyota's actual baseline

Every reference figure in §3–§5 above is a function of three Toyota-specific inputs: (a) actual vehicle volume per affected build window, (b) actual warranty cost per vehicle by program, and (c) actual chargeback recovery rate over the prior 12–24 months. 09What this document is not

  • Not a proposal. No scope of work, no fee, no timeline.
